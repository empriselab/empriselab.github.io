SoNIC Workshop

About SoNIC

During the weeklong SoNIC Summer Research Workshop, participants learn about a variety of state-of-the-art computing research directions happening at Cornell University and beyond, and get an opportunity to conduct a short research project. Participants will also learn about research-oriented career paths in computing, including the process of obtaining advanced degrees. More information about the next offering can be found on the Cornell DEI website.

Course Project and Materials

The workshop will focus on robotics and assistive technology. Specifically, students will work together to build Smart Assistive Canes for Blind and Low Vision Users.

Demonstration

Video description: A researcher holds the smart cane before him while covering his eyes. He takes small steps forward, then turns to his right to avoid an obstacle (a stack of clear boxes). He takes a few more steps, turns to his left, then walks forward again.

Technical Details

Smart Assistive Cane Execution

Video description: Multiple views (top-down and highlighted) of the researcher navigating around the obstacles shown in the previous video presented in Rviz, a 3D visualizer for the Robot Operating System framework. In one of the views, the boxes have a black and white fiducial marker on top and are highlighted in yellow, and the cane has a fiducial marker on top and is highlighted in red.

System Overview

Image description: A diagram showing the world model on the left and bidirectional arrows between the world model and the control system. The control system has a cycle with an arrow pointing to the vibration motor, actions such as 'move left, right, in front, and beep', another arrow pointing to the entire smart cane, another arrow pointing to the distance sensor and camera, and another arrow pointing back to the control system.
